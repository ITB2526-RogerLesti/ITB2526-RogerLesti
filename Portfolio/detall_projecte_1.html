ANALYSIS: MICROORGANISMS IN OUR ENVIRONMENT

DETAILED RESEARCH REPORT: HYGIENE EFFICACY STUDY

This research project focused on analyzing the presence and proliferation of microorganisms, primarily bacteria, found on commonly used surfaces within a secondary school environment.

The core objective was to quantify bacterial colonies on surfaces frequently handled by students and teachers to raise awareness about hygiene risks and evaluate the efficacy of current cleaning practices.

1. EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN & METHODOLOGY

The practical component involved conducting seven distinct culturing tests using Agar-Agar plates. Each test compared microbial samples taken from a "dirty" side of a surface versus a "clean" side (cleaned immediately before sampling).

Surfaces sampled included: mobile phones, hands, laboratory tables, door handles, photocopiers, computer keyboards, and the school bathroom.

2. KEY FINDINGS AND ANALYSIS

The results confirmed a significant presence of microbial life, with the uncleaned samples from the men's bathroom showing the highest number of colonies by far.

Crucially, the comparison demonstrated that a thorough cleaning using soap and detergent was highly effective, often resulting in zero bacterial colonies on the "clean" side of the Petri dish.

The analysis highlighted mobile phones and hands as the most critical points of hygiene, given their direct pathway to the body via the oral route.

3. CONCLUSION & RECOMMENDATIONS

The hypothesis—questioning the sufficiency of habitual hygiene measures—was partially validated; while cleaning is effective, the high initial contamination suggests inconsistent or insufficient daily practice.

The final recommendation urges individuals to take proactive responsibility for maintaining the cleanliness of their immediate work areas and personal items to prevent microbial imbalances and potential health risks.

RELATED THEORETICAL CONCEPTS:

  • Microorganisms: Defined as microscopic living beings, including Bacteria, Archaea, and Eukarya, that perform vital functions.
  • Beneficial Microbes: Discussion included probiotic bacteria (e.g., Lactobacillus) that aid digestion and strengthen intestinal immunity.
  • Patogenic Risks: Outlines how pathogens (viruses, bacteria, fungi) cause disease by invading the body and emphasizes that risk depends on immune system health.
