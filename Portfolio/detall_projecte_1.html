ANALYSIS: MICROORGANISMS IN OUR ENVIRONMENT

DETAILED SEARCH REPORT

This research work focuses on the study of microorganisms, especially bacteria, found in the everyday environment and how they influence daily life.

The main objective is to demonstrate the number of bacterial colonies present on the surfaces most used by students and teachers in a secondary school.

The hypothesis raises the question of whether the usual hygiene measures people use are effective enough to lead a healthy life.

The practical part involves culturing samples of "dirty" and "clean" surfaces (such as phones, hands, or the bathroom) in Petri dishes to count and compare bacterial proliferation.

The findings suggest that the hygiene measures adopted by people are not entirely effective, recommending more frequent cleaning of work areas and personal items to avoid microbial imbalances.

PROTOCOLS AND RESULTS:

  • E-01: Cultivation in Petri dishes (Agar-Agar)
  • E-02: Comparative analysis of colony density
  • E-03: Web design (Digital presentation)
