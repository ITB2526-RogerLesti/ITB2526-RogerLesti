Office Prevention Project

DETAILED REPORT SUMMARY

This project focuses on the complete and detailed design of a **Prevention Plan and an Emergency Plan** for a specific working environment (an office or work center), ensuring compliance with current Occupational Health and Safety regulations.

The main objective is twofold: **1) Prevent** common occupational risks by identifying and applying necessary corrective measures, and **2) Establish** protocols for rapid and organized action in the event of an emergency (such as fire, electrical failure, or medical incident).

1. PREVENTION PLAN ANALYSIS (Part A)

The Prevention Plan outlines the office's profile, the chosen prevention method, and the identification of three main risks inherent to the activity. It specifies the preventative measures applied:

  • Organizational Measure: (e.g., scheduled safety inspections and procedure reviews).
  • Material Measure: (e.g., installation of collective protection elements like railings or safety signage).
  • Training Measure: (e.g., annual staff training courses on fire extinguisher use or first aid).

2. EMERGENCY PLAN STRATEGY (Part B)

This section classifies the emergency risks (minor incident, partial, general emergency) and defines the action procedures (alarm, evacuation, confinement). Key emergency roles are established, such as the Emergency Chief and the Intervention Chief, assigning specific functions to each team.

3. TECHNICAL INFRASTRUCTURE (Part D: The Data Center)

A critical analysis of security measures for a **Data Processing Center (DPC/CPD)** is included, highlighting why specific controls are essential:

  • Temperature Control: To prevent server overheating, which causes hardware failure and drastically shortens equipment lifespan.
  • Electrical Overload Avoidance: To mitigate fire risk and prevent data loss/corruption from sudden shutdowns.
  • UPS Use (Uninterruptible Power Supply): For electrical protection and providing temporary reserve power for safe server shutdown during power cuts.

This project demonstrates the ability to analyze and design complex safety structures to ensure business continuity and protect personnel and technological assets.
