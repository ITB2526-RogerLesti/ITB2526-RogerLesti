ITB Sustainability Analysis

🌿 Environmental (E)

  • • High electricity consumption in systems classrooms and servers.
  • • Generation of electronic waste (e-waste) due to obsolete hardware.

👥 Social (S)

  • • Student ergonomics and digital eye strain.
  • • Inclusion by reducing the digital divide.

⚖️ Governance (G)

  • • Ethics in data management and privacy.
  • • Promotion of Free Software (Open Source) at the center.

♻️
WEEE Waste Management (RD 110/2015)

1

Selective Collection

Strict separation of cables, screens, and boards. Never mixed with common waste.

2

Secure Storage

Dry spaces for batteries. Avoid dismantling without protection.

3

Certified Management

Delivery to authorized managers for legal traceability.

Big Tech Comparison & Critique

Company Commitment Improvement Proposal (Group 7)

Implementation of the "Reuse Workshop": A student-managed laboratory to repair ITB hardware. This promotes the "Right to Repair" and directly reduces the center's carbon footprint.
