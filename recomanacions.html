Roadmap 2026-2029

Impact Reduction Strategy

To reduce ITB's environmental impact by 30% in just three years, we have decided to move away from superficial solutions and tackle the root of the problem: the technological consumption model. Our plan is not just a list of good intentions; it is a paradigm shift based on radical circularity.

1
Year 1: Efficiency

Audit and Sensors

We will transform the institute into a smart center through IoT monitoring that eradicates invisible electrical waste.

2
Year 2: Circularity

Reuse Workshop & Right to Repair

We will take the big leap where we, the systems students, will transform electronic waste into real value, applying the "Right to Repair" to extend the lifespan of hardware and curb the purchase of new equipment.

3
3
Year 3: Independence

Renewable Energies and Water Optimization

We will close the circle by optimizing every drop of water with rain collection systems and high-efficiency aerators.

Strategy Details

🎯 Measurable Objectives

Certified 30% reduction in electricity, water, and paper consumption bills within a 36-month period.

📊 Indicators (KPIs)

kWh/student, m³/month, kg of recycled paper, and kg of CO2 saved (calculated by our JS engine).

♻️ Circular Economy

We apply the "R's" of Reuse and Repair instead of the linearity of "buy-use-dispose," turning obsolete equipment into educational resources.

📉 Technical Quantification

If you simulate the plan with the calculator, you will see how the "School Year" cards automatically switch to show the reduced value (0.70 factor).
